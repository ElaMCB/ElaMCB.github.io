A Framework Is Not a Values Poster: What Teams Should Document

If it does not change a ticket, a test, or a sign-off, it is wallpaper. Here is the minimum structure that counts as a framework for shipping AI.

Three artifacts that make it real

A useful ethical AI framework ties abstract principles to decisions someone actually makes: who approves a new model or feature, what evidence they need, and what happens when a check fails. Without those hooks, "be fair" and "respect privacy" are slogans, not engineering constraints.

Start with a one-page decision map: intake (idea or model change), owners (named roles, not "the org"), required reviews (bias, privacy, safety, human oversight), and exit criteria for production. That map is your framework in practice; the glossy diagram is optional.

Why posters still appear

Leadership often asks for visible ethics commitments before process exists. That is fine as long as you file the poster next to a living checklist in Jira or your release tool. The LinkedIn audience for ethical AI frameworks usually wants both: language they can rally around and mechanics they can audit.
