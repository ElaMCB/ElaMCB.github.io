Human Override Rules for AI Systems: Who Can Stop What, and When

Define clear override authority and escalation paths before production pressure makes decisions for you.

Every AI workflow needs a brake pedal

Ethical AI frameworks are incomplete without explicit override authority. If nobody knows who can disable a model behavior during an incident, your governance exists only on slides.

Write override rules in role language: Support lead can disable feature flag X, Incident commander can block tool Y, Product owner can restrict geography Z.

Design the escalation ladder

Document three levels: immediate containment (minutes), temporary mitigations (hours), and long-term corrective actions (days). Assign owners and communication channels for each level.

Include customer-facing messaging ownership so public comms do not lag engineering decisions.

Practice before you need it

Run one tabletop exercise per quarter on a realistic failure mode. Measure decision latency and handoff clarity, not just technical fixes.

Teams that rehearse overrides recover faster and make fewer ad-hoc policy decisions under pressure.
