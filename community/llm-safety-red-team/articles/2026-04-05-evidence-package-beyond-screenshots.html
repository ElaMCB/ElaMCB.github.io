The Red-Team Evidence Package: What to Log Beyond Screenshots

QA leaders need artifacts Security and Legal can reuse. Here is a minimum viable logging checklist for one adversarial session.

Why screenshots fail as evidence

A screenshot proves what someone saw once. It does not prove which model version, temperature, retrieval context, or tool schema was active. For production gates and post-incident review, you need structured fields that stay comparable across releases.

Minimum fields per prompt attempt

Store: UTC timestamp, environment (prod vs staging), deployment or model ID, full user-visible transcript including system/developer messages if your harness exposes them, retrieved chunks or tool payloads (redacted per policy), latency, and a hash or run_id tying the row to your spreadsheet gate. If the model refuses, store the refusal text verbatim—regressions often show up as refusals becoming compliances.

Handoff to Security

Export a single JSON or CSV bundle per sprint with stable prompt_id keys matching your gate matrix. Security can then replay in a sandbox or map findings to CWE-style categories without re-asking QA for missing columns.
